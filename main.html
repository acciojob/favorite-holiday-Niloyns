Kolkata

Kolkata (formerly Calcutta) is the capital of India's West Bengal state. Founded as an East India Company trading post, it was India's capital under the British Raj from 1773–1911. Today it’s known for its grand colonial architecture, art galleries and cultural festivals. It’s also home to Mother House, headquarters of the Missionaries of Charity, founded by Mother Teresa, whose tomb is on site.

  1. Victoria Memorial — a grand marble monument symbolizing British-era heritage and art.
  2. Howrah Bridge — the iconic cantilever bridge connecting Kolkata and Howrah.
  3. Indian Museum — India’s oldest museum with rare artifacts and fossils.
  4. Dakshineswar Kali Temple — a sacred Hindu temple dedicated to Goddess Kali.
  5. Eco Park — a vast urban park offering boating, cycling, and themed gardens.
  • Rosogolla — soft and spongy syrup-soaked Bengali sweet loved by everyone.
  • Macher Jhol — a traditional fish curry cooked with spices and vegetables.
  • Kathi Roll — Kolkata’s famous street food wrap filled with egg, meat, or paneer.
  • Phuchka — crispy puris filled with spicy tamarind water and mashed potato.
  • Mishti Doi — sweetened fermented yogurt served chilled for dessert.
